# Entry Signal Principles

Synthesized from: O'Neil (CAN SLIM), Minervini (SEPA), Livermore, Lynch.
These rules govern WHEN to buy, not just WHAT to buy.

---

## Market Conditions First (O'Neil, PTJ)

1. Only enter new longs during a confirmed market uptrend. A correction is not the time to buy breakouts — you are fighting the tide.
2. SPY above its 200-day MA is the minimum bar for trend-following strategies. Below it, stick to mean reversion or stay flat.
3. Count distribution days on the index. Four or more distribution days in a three-week window = back off new buys.
4. A follow-through day (index up 1.5%+ on higher volume after a rally attempt) confirms a new uptrend. That is the green light.

## Stock Selection (Minervini SEPA, O'Neil)

5. Price must be above its 50-day, 150-day, and 200-day SMAs — in that order. Below any of these = Stage 1 or Stage 4, not buyable.
6. The 150-day SMA must be above the 200-day SMA (trend alignment).
7. The 200-day SMA must be rising for at least one month. Flat or declining 200-day = avoid.
8. Stock is within 25% of its 52-week high. Buyable setups come from strength, not bargain hunting.
9. Relative strength vs. SPY should be positive over the last 3 and 6 months. You want leaders, not laggards.

## The Base (O'Neil)

10. Best entries come from stocks forming proper consolidation bases: cup-with-handle, flat base, or tight area of price contraction.
11. A base is valid when the stock consolidates for at least 3 weeks with declining volume.
12. Lower base counts (1st or 2nd base) are safer than late-stage bases (3rd, 4th) — the move is fresher.
13. Buy within 5% of the pivot point, not extended beyond it. Chasing is the enemy.

## Volume Confirmation (O'Neil)

14. A breakout from a base on volume at least 50% above the 50-day average volume is institutional confirmation.
15. A breakout on weak volume is a warning. Wait for a second attempt or skip it.
16. Accumulation days (up on above-average volume) inside the base show institutional buying. Count them.

## Technicals at Entry (our strategy filters)

17. RSI(14) between 40 and 70 at entry. Below 40 = potential breakdown. Above 70 = extended, chasing.
18. MA crossover (10-day above 30-day) confirms short-term trend momentum.
19. Mean reversion entries: only when price tags the lower Bollinger Band AND the 200-day MA is rising. A breakdown can look like reversion.

## What to Skip

20. Skip any buy signal when the news sentiment score for that ticker is below -0.3 (bearish). Fundamentals matter even for technical traders.
21. Skip earnings-week entries unless the setup is extraordinarily clear. Earnings are a binary event, not a thesis.
22. Skip thinly traded stocks. Minimum 500k shares/day average volume — you need liquidity to exit.
